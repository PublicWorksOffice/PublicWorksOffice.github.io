Public Works Office

Public Works Office (PwO) exists to "make technology public"1: open, interoperable, inclusive, and user-centric.

PwO is a technical consultancy that provides advice related to software product development, technology integration and digital customer experience.

PwO has particular experience with open source (the integration of, as well as developing new communities), systems architecture based on open standards and interfaces, and transportation: trip planners, real-time arrival prediction systems, customer experience, digital signage and content, and data.

At PwO, we believe people come together to do great, big things as one "body". Government is one of the best examples, and we're here to help those on the inside working on great, big things.

Past Work

  • Actionable roadmap to leverage digital advertising screens to improve customer communications and serve double-duty at LA Metro: growing and retaining ridership, while generating new revenue.

  • Technology product vision for a next generation city built "from the Internet up". Presented to the Alphabet board and submitted, in part, as Sidewalk Labs' vision for Sidewalk Toronto.

  • Pilot kiosk-based program to connect veterans with transportation and economic opportunity in York, PA. Part of USDOT's Veterans Transportation and Community Living Initiative (VTCLI). With Cambridge Systematics.

  • A custom route planner designed to route users through as many "sidewalk sheds" as possible to avoid sun, rain or other adverse environmental conditions. Presented as part of PwO's talk titled "Against Efficiency" at Conflux 2012.

  • A machine-learning powered system to match buses observed on the streets of New York City to the bus schedule, allowing realtime arrival predictions to be given to customers. With OpenPlans and Cambridge Systematics.

  • Pre-acquisition advisory work on scaling, reliability and transition of New York City's bikeshare. With Control Group.

  • A 15-week investigation into "Who Decides Where The Subway Goes?" with New York City high-school students. With CUP, as PwO with Alexandra Woolsey-Puffer.

  • Deployment of OpenTripPlanner for Sound Transit in Seattle, including a customized web front-end built on the OpenGeo stack.

  • A Flickr-like website to share gigapixel+ images, taken by a unique camera rig (shown above). Includes software to take, stitch and upload images to the web. (Photo by Alan Levine)

  • A robotic visualization of the aural landscape of Pittsburgh, collected via MapHub and displayed in Karlsruhe Germany. Exhibited at ZKM's exhibit "Making Things Public" in 2005.

  • A wiki-like, community-editable geographic information system (GIS) to encourage grassroots neighborhood data sharing to encourage more equitable, inclusive development.

  • Web-based decision support and incident response system for emergency responders in Western Pennsylvania (FEMA Region 13).

PwO's Values (draft)
  1. Understand the details. The details are often where opportunity lies: to do something new, to do something faster, to accomplish the goal you set out to achieve.
  2. Strengthen our clients: like a good camper, leave a place better than you found it. Progress breeds more opportunity.
  3. Be honest--it's okay to disagree. The truth always wins in the end, why ignore it? Develop a hypothesis about the world and test it.
  4. Be thoughtful: most often this means helping our clients make the best decision for them given constraints. Nothing is perfect, and nothing is final.

1: "public" in the sense that things encode values in their design. Communiites of experts that make value-laden decisions are not typically venues for engagement by the general public/the entire "body" of people affected by the decisions made.
